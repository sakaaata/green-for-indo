Problem Exploration


Indonesia is the world’s third greatest emitter of greenhouse gasses. The main contributor to Indonesia’s high release of greenhouse gasses is mainly due to forestry. It accounts for approximately 2,563 million tons (Mt) of CO2-equivalent greenhouse gasses (CO2e). Within this sector, the most significant carbon dioxide emissions arise from deforestation and land conversion, with 75 percent.  Indonesia foresees reaching the peak of national GHGs emissions in 2030 with a net sink in forestry and land uses (FOLU), and with further exploring opportunities to rapidly progress towards net-zero emission in 2060 or sooner.
In order to reduce carbon emissions by 41 percent by 2030, Reforestation is one of the main Indonesian government’s key tools for achieving a sustainable future. This commitment is included in the Golden Indonesia 2045 Program. With an average annual decline in emissions of 30.7 MTon CO2.


Our Objective is to help Reforestation, while also sustain local Socioeconomic and Reserve an endangered endemic flora, named Dyrobalanops Aromatica.


Why Dryobalanops Aromatica?


The potential for the high-value economic value of camphor tree Dryobalanops Sumatrensis (J.F.Gmelin) results in a large number of exploitation and illegal logging occurring, Its existence is increasingly threatened due to land use changes and illegal logging leading to the urgent need for conservation actions in the field. 

IUCN has granted Vulnerable Endangered status to Dryobalanops Sumatrensis since 2018. Therefore, in-situ and ex-situ conservation efforts need to be made to maintain the existence of this species. However, planting stock production is still problematic due to irregular flowering and fruiting. Since fruiting of Dryobalanops Aromatica is irregular, it is necessary to produce a large quantity of planting material for the reforestation program.

Current status of Research Investigation

The proposed program was initiated to develop methods for vegetative propagation of this species, by conventional as well as biotechnological approaches. The conservation program started in February last year when the school gracefully received 2 Dryobalanops Aromatica trees from the Silviculture department, Faculty of Forestry, Bogor Agricultural University (IPB).

Two weeks later, on February 21st, 2021, a Propagation by stem cutting was commenced, the media used were sand, rice husk media, and a combination of both media aiming to evaluate the survival rate, rooting, and shoot sprouting abilities of D. Dryobalanops. However the study was not successful, it was believed that the tree trunks used were still too small since the Dryobalanops tree was not mature yet  

First Approach

A year later one of the trees has grown to 3,2 meters tall and then it would be appropriate to do another propagation by stem cutting again. Using 4 types of media and 3 types of hormone, the survival rate, rooting and shootsprouting abilities of D. Dryobalanops are once again observed. The process is promising some of the stems have grown roots and it's an ongoing process,

Second Approach

Second approach in propagation is using Vegetative propagation by stem cutting and conducted inside aseptic tissue culture materials at the Plant Tissue Culture Laboratory of TechnoNatura. Dryobalanops Sumatrensis aged less than 3 years old were used as cutting materials. Two different tissue culture materials WPM, MS were used in this study. And 4 kinds of hormones were used. B1/Thiamin, Gibberellin, BAP and with a number combination approach. And for surface sterilization a numbermethods of sterilization were deployed, Results from the study is still undergoing experimentation. Further observation is required to support the complete data.

Third Approach

The third approach is by Cloning Dryobalanops Aromatica by means of tissue culture. Though more difficult as compared to conventional propagation, cloning by means of tissue culture has many advantages. One is for the production of a large quantity of planting material for the reforestation programand second is the rapid multiplication of selected clones in the tree improvement program.  Three different tissue culture materials WPM, MS and Gamborg were used. And 4 types of hormones were used. B1/Thiamin, Gibberellin, BAP and with a number combination approach for surface sterilization, The results from the study are not yet finalized and further data required to support the complete research.
